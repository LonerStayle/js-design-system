Spring foraging walk — Wytham Woods, 11 April

FG
Fenwood Garden Trust <walks@fenwoodtrust.org>
to Members list · 21 March 2026, 8:02
Verdant Society — Events

Spring has crept into the understorey, and the woodland floor is greening fast. Join curator Bramble Ashford for a gentle three-hour ramble through the ancient oaks of Wytham Woods in search of the season's first wild edibles.

We'll be looking for ramsons (Allium ursinum) carpeting the damp hollows, the bittercress and wood sorrel of the path edges, and the unfurling fiddleheads of Matteuccia struthiopteris. Bramble will cover responsible foraging — taking only a leaf in ten, never the bulb, and always leaving the rarest plants untouched.

Stout boots and a basket are advised; identification cards will be provided. Places are limited to fifteen so the woodland is not over-trodden.

Allium ursinum wild garlic · sat 11 apr · 10am · meet at the gate

Yours in leaf,
The Fenwood Garden Trust

Elderflower cordial — a forager's reminder

CB
Bramble Ashford <bramble@verdant.society>
to Members list · 18 March 2026
Verdant Society — Foraging notes

A few notes before the blossom turns, since the hedgerows are nearly ready. Gather Sambucus nigra on a dry, sunny morning when the tiny florets are fully open and creamy — never after rain, which washes away the scented pollen that carries all the flavour.

Snip whole heads with a little stalk, shake out any insects rather than washing, and steep the same day with lemon and sugar. Take only the lower flowers and leave plenty for the bees and the autumn berries.

I'll bring a bottle of last year's cordial to the April walk for anyone curious.

In good leaf,
Bramble — Society curator
