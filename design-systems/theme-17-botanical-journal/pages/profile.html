A note from the curator

I came to ferns the slow way — kneeling on wet basalt with a hand lens, trying to tell one delicate frond from another.

Eleven years on, the journal's fern archive holds more than twelve hundred pressed sheets, each keyed, mounted, and cross-referenced against the regional flora. My work focuses on the pteridophytes of Cascadia: the sword ferns that shingle the forest floor, the maidenhairs that hang from seeping cliffs, and the licorice ferns that ride the mossy backs of bigleaf maples. Every sheet here carries a locality, a collection date, and an honest note on how confident I am about the determination.

If you are new to keying ferns, message me — I keep a running set of field notes on spore arrangement and frond dimorphism, and I am always glad to look over a tricky sheet.
