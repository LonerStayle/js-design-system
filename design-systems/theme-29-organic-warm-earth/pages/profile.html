The studio story

Terraforma started in a converted barn outside Gyeongju in 2019, with one secondhand wheel and a kiln Soyeon rebuilt brick by brick. The first season was all failures — bowls that cracked in the cooling, glazes that ran clean off the rim — but the clay underfoot was good, and she kept throwing.

Today every piece is still made by hand from a single batch of local stoneware, wedged, thrown, trimmed and wood-fired over three days. The ash that settles during the firing becomes part of the glaze, which is why no two mugs ever leave the studio quite alike. That unevenness isn't a flaw to her — it's the whole point.

The studio ships small, numbered runs a few times a year. When a collection sells out, it's gone; the next one will look a little different, fired a little hotter, leaning into whatever the kiln decides to give back.
