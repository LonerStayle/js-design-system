About Eleanor

Biography

Eleanor Hartwell has spent eleven years on the politics beat, eight of them at The Ledger, where she covers the legislature, the campaign trail, and the slow machinery of governance that rarely makes the front page but quietly shapes the year. She is known in the newsroom for filing clean copy on impossible deadlines and for keeping a source list that predates most of the building.

Before joining the paper she reported on city hall for a regional daily, where she broke a contracting scandal that led to two indictments and a change in procurement law. She believes the best political reporting is procedural — that the vote you do not see coming is usually decided in a committee room weeks earlier.

Off the clock she teaches a seminar on investigative method, mentors two junior reporters on the desk, and maintains, with some pride, the newsroom’s most exhaustive archive of floor schedules.
