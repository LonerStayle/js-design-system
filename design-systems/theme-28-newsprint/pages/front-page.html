Senate Passes Sweeping Reform After All-Night Session

In a 58–41 vote just before dawn, lawmakers approved the most significant overhaul in a generation, sending the measure to the desk of a president who has promised to sign it within the week.

By Margaret Reyes & D. Okonkwo Washington — 4:12 a.m. Developing

The vote capped a week of furious negotiation that spilled past midnight and into the small hours, with leaders from both benches trading concessions on the floor while staff shuttled redlined pages between offices. By the time the clerk called the roll, the outcome was no longer in doubt, but the margin surprised even seasoned observers.

“This is what the institution looks like when it works,” said the majority leader, flanked by colleagues outside the chamber. Critics were less generous, warning that the bill’s costs had been understated and its timelines left deliberately vague.

A Long Road

The measure had been declared dead twice this season, once in committee and again on a procedural motion that failed by a single vote. Its revival owed much to a quiet bloc of moderates who extracted late amendments on funding and oversight.
