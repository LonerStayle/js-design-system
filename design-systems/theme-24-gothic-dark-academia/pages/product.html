On the Art of the Scrivener's Instrument

"The pen is the tongue of the mind." — Miguel de Cervantes, 1605

In the year of Our Lord MDCCCXII, Josiah Locke and Thomas Quill — former apprentices to the esteemed Bramble & Son of Fleet Street — established their atelier in a narrow passage off St. Paul's Churchyard. Their founding creed, inscribed still above the workshop door, reads Scribe ut meminisse possis: write that you may remember. Two centuries on, that philosophy endures in every instrument that bears the mark of the crossed quill.

The Oxford Scrivener represents the fourteenth collector's edition in an unbroken tradition of limited commissions, each named in homage to a seat of scholarship. Where its predecessors evoked Cambridge, Edinburgh, and the Sorbonne, this edition pays tribute to the Bodleian Library — that great repository of two million volumes — whose Reading Room served as the spiritual crucible for its design. The deep Burgundy Ebonite recalls the Morocco-leather spines of incunabula; the gold nib, the gilded rubrics of illuminated manuscripts.

A Note on the Nib

Each nib is formed from 18-carat two-tone gold — 750/1000 fineness — by hand at our London workshop, requiring upward of four hours per piece. The iridium-tipped tines are spread and burnished by eye under magnification, achieving a flow that Pensmith Whitmore-Crain describes as "a controlled surrender of the ink to the page."

The piston-fill mechanism, machined in German steel, draws 1.2 millilitres of ink with a single rotation — sufficient for three to four pages of uninterrupted correspondence in Medium or a full five in Fine. The pen accepts standard international cartridges as well as the supplied converter, and is compatible with all iron-gall, carbon, and standard dye-based inks. We recommend our own Oxford Iron Gall blend for optimal flow and permanence.

The Oxford Scrivener is presented in a hand-stitched burgundy leather case, lined with bevelled mahogany and midnight velvet. Enclosed are: a glass ink sample, a certificate of provenance bearing the edition number and pensmith's signature, a copy of On the Care of Fine Pens (Locke & Quill, MMIV), and a voucher for the complimentary first-year nib-tuning service at our Jermyn Street studio.
