Biographical Précis

Evander Blackthorn holds the Regius Chair of Palaeography and Textual Criticism at Athenaeum College, Oxford — the oldest continuous fellowship in manuscript scholarship within the University. His principal researches concern the transmission of Classical texts through Carolingian scriptoria, with particular attention to the marginalia, colophons, and interlinear glosses that reveal the intellectual lives of anonymous monastic copyists from the IXth and Xth centuries.

He is the author of four monographs, most recently Ink Upon Vellum: Voices at the Margin of the Medieval Text (Oxford University Press, MMXXIII), and has edited the standard critical edition of the Pseudo-Alcuinian Quaestiones Grammaticae. He serves on the editorial boards of Scriptorium, The Library, and Medium Ævum.
