Frequently Asked Questions

Quod nescis, quaere — What thou knowest not, enquire.

Indeed — all marginalia, bookmarks, and the contents of your private commonplace book are preserved in their entirety upon elevation to any higher tier. Your accumulated scholarship belongs to you; the Library merely grants you further dominion over its stacks. Upgrading takes effect immediately upon confirmation of your new membership, with any unused portion of the previous billing period credited to your account in the form of extended tenure.
The Open Stacks contain the greater body of the modern and late-modern collection — works published from the XVIII century onward, scholarly journals, critical editions, and contemporary monographs. The Restricted Alcoves house materials of considerably greater antiquity and fragility: illuminated manuscripts, incunabula (those printed before the year MD, i.e. 1500), rare cartographical atlases, alchemical treatises, and the ecclesiastical correspondences of the XIII and XIV centuries. Access requires a Fellow or Curator membership to ensure proper scholarly custody of these irreplaceable works.
We confess no trickery. Annual billing is offered at the equivalent of ten monthly instalments — meaning you receive the equivalent of two months' membership entirely gratis. For the Scholar, of course, the matter is of no financial consequence. For the Fellow, this represents a saving of £24 per annum; for the Curator, a saving of £58. One may think of it as the Library's gratitude for your sustained patronage and your confidence in us across the full academic year. Annual memberships are issued a single invoice and renew automatically unless cancelled prior to the renewal date.
We do, and warmly encourage such arrangements. Institutions wishing to provide access for their students, fellows, or faculty members — whether a college of the ancient universities, a learned society, a seminary, or an independent research institute — are invited to address their enquiries directly to our Archivist via the correspondence channel. Institutional arrangements typically begin at ten seats and include bespoke onboarding, SAML / SSO integration, consolidated invoicing, and a dedicated liaison. Discounts are negotiated individually; however, they are genuinely substantial.
Upon expiry, your membership reverts gracefully to the Scholar tier — the Library's outer doors remain open to you always. Access to the Alcoves, Scriptorium, and any tier-specific collections is suspended, but your reading history, bookmarks, and up to 500 annotations are preserved indefinitely in your account. Any facsimiles already downloaded remain in your possession. Should you choose to return — and the Library hopes sincerely that you shall — your full commonplace book and annotation history will be restored immediately upon resubscription.
The Founders' Colloquium is an annual gathering — held each October in proximity to the feast of St. Jerome, patron of scholars — at which Curator members are invited to convene in person or via our private correspondence salon. The occasion comprises: a reading of selected acquisitions from the preceding year, a philosophical discourse led by an invited Fellow of the Bodleian or Bibliothèque nationale, the annual report of the Library's Acquisitions Committee, and a candlelit supper during which members may address the Curators on matters of collection development. Dress is appropriately scholarly. Latin is welcomed but not obligatory.
