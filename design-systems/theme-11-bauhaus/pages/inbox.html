SL
Sophie Laurent

Today, 10:42 AM

Hi team,

I've attached the revised component specifications from this week's Q3 design review session. We covered a lot of ground — button states, form field variants, and the updated color token mapping from the Bauhaus audit.

Key feedback from the review:

1. Primary buttons — The flat shadow offset should remain at 3px across all breakpoints. No gradient, ever. The Bauhaus principle of honest materials applies here too.

2. Form inputs — Error states need both a color change (red border) and a shape cue (the error icon). Relying on color alone fails WCAG 1.4.1. I've added the inline-note component to the field error pattern.

3. Typography — The display class at 64px feels strong on desktop. On mobile (< 480px), cap it at 40px. The Jost typeface handles the weight well at both sizes.

Please review the attached specs by end of day Thursday and leave your comments in Figma. I'll consolidate everything before the Friday handoff to engineering.

Looking forward to your thoughts,
Sophie

Hey,

Sharing the updated WCAG 2.2 checklist we discussed at last week's all-hands. I've gone through each component in the current design system and flagged issues.

Focus indicators: The default browser outline is being suppressed in base.css without an equivalent replacement. This fails 2.4.11 (Focus Appearance). We need a visible 2px offset ring on all interactive elements.

Color contrast: Yellow backgrounds with white text are a hard fail at 1.07:1. Confirmed the design spec now mandates black text on yellow (`--color-text-on-accent`). Good.

Touch targets: Several icon buttons are 32×32px — below the WCAG 2.2 minimum of 24×24px. The `.btn-icon` class needs a minimum 44×44px clickable area via padding.

Full annotated checklist attached. Happy to do a walk-through session.

Hey,

I love the 12-column base — it gives us the asymmetric layout freedom that's core to the Bauhaus aesthetic. But I think we should seriously explore a 4-column fallback for mobile.

12 columns at 375px with 16px gutters leaves only about 17px per column — barely usable. A 4-column grid at mobile gives us proper breathing room and still supports the modular structure.

I've mocked up a few screens at both breakpoints. The 4-col version looks much cleaner and stays true to the rational, geometric approach. Happy to share the Figma file if useful.
