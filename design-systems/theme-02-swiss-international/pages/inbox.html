Hi — thanks for turning round the third pass so quickly. I have walked the whole wayfinding set on the 200×200 module and the structure is holding beautifully. The decision to lock everything to the eight-pixel baseline has paid off: nothing floats, every label sits exactly where the eye expects it.

Two things before we send plates to the printer on Friday. First, the directional arrows read a touch light against the concrete; bumping the rule from one to two pixels closes the gap without resorting to a heavier weight. Second, the floor-level signage wants one more step of type contrast — the current 22pt feels timid at three metres. Try the 28pt display step and tighten tracking to −0.02em.

Budget-wise we are on track. The fabrication quote came back at €48,000, which lands inside the contingency we set in March. I have attached the marked-up proof and the latest cost sheet so you have both in one place.

If you can confirm the two changes by Thursday noon, the print house will hold our Friday slot. Otherwise we slip a week, which I would rather avoid given the exhibition opening.

Best,
Anni
