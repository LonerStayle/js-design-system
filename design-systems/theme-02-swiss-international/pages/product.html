The Helvetica Wall Clock distils the dial to its essentials: a clean white-on-black face, Helvetica numerals at the cardinal hours, and a single quiet red second hand — the one place colour moves.

RASTER turns each case from a solid billet of aluminium, then hard-anodises the surface for a matte, fingerprint-resistant finish that holds its colour for decades. The bezel is a continuous 4 mm hairline; there is no applied ring, no bevel, no ornament. What you see is the structure of the object itself.

Inside sits a German-engineered continuous-sweep quartz movement. There is no tick — the second hand glides, silent enough for a bedroom or a recording studio. A single AA cell drives it for roughly eighteen months.

The typography follows the lineage of the Zürich and Basel schools: objective, gridded, and legible across a room. Numerals are set at a 7.5° optical correction so the face reads true from below, the way a wall clock is actually seen.

Designed and assembled in Winterthur, Switzerland. Each unit is inspected and numbered before dispatch.
